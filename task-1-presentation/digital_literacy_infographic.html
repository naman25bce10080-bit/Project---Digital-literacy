Your guide to 5 Essential Skills
What it is

Digital Literacy is the ability to find, evaluate, create, and communicate information using digital technology — confidently, critically, and responsibly. It's not just about using devices; it's about using them wisely.

1 🛠️
Useful Digital Tools
For students
Notion – notes & planning Google Scholar – research Canva – design & slides Zotero – citations Grammarly – writing Trello – project tasks Zoom / Meet – collaboration
Pro tip: Use your college email to unlock free Pro tiers on many platforms — Notion, Figma, GitHub, and more.
2 🔒
Safe Internet Practices
Protect yourself online
Use strong, unique passwords Enable 2-Factor Authentication Spot phishing emails Use HTTPS sites only Limit personal data sharing Update software regularly Use a VPN on public Wi-Fi
Remember: Think before you click. If a link, email, or offer feels off — it probably is.
3 🌐
Professional Online Presence
Build your digital identity
Complete LinkedIn profile Professional profile photo Portfolio / GitHub page Google yourself regularly Clean up old social posts Use a professional username
Key insight: Recruiters Google you before interviews. Your online presence is your first impression — make it count.
4 ✉️
Email Etiquette
Write like a professional
✅ Do This
✍️
Clear subject line "Assignment 3 Query – John, CS2B"
👋
Proper greeting "Dear Prof. Sharma," or "Hello Sir/Ma'am,"
🎯
Be concise & specific State your request in 2–3 sentences
🙏
Sign off politely "Warm regards, [Name, Roll No.]"
❌ Avoid This
🚫
Vague subjects "hi", "help", or blank subject lines
📵
Texting language "pls send notes asap ty 🙏" is not okay
😤
Demanding tone Always ask, never demand or guilt-trip
🔁
Chasing too fast Wait 48 hrs before a polite follow-up
Be smart, be safe, be digital. 🚀
