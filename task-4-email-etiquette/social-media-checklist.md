# Social Media Checklist for College Students
### Responsible Use Guide

---

## ✅ DO's

| # | Do This |
|---|---------|
| 1 | **Think before you post** — Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor, employer, or family saw this?" If not, don't post it. |
| 2 | **Keep your profiles professional** — Use LinkedIn and GitHub to showcase your skills, certifications, and academic projects for future employers. |
| 3 | **Protect your privacy settings** — Regularly review and update the privacy settings on all your platforms to control who sees your content. |
| 4 | **Give credit where it's due** — Always acknowledge original creators when sharing, reposting, or quoting someone else's work or ideas. |
| 5 | **Use social media for learning** — Follow industry leaders, academic pages, and professional communities relevant to your field of study. |
| 6 | **Set time limits** — Use built-in screen time tools to manage how long you spend on social media so it doesn't interfere with your studies. |

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

| # | Avoid This |
|---|------------|
| 1 | **Don't share personal information publicly** — Avoid posting your home address, phone number, daily schedule, or financial details online. |
| 2 | **Don't engage in cyberbullying or online harassment** — Never post hateful, discriminatory, or degrading content about peers, professors, or institutions. |
| 3 | **Don't post during class or exams** — Scrolling through social media during lectures undermines your learning and shows disrespect to your educators. |
| 4 | **Don't spread unverified information** — Always fact-check before sharing news, statistics, or claims — misinformation spreads fast and can harm your credibility. |
| 5 | **Don't post about others without consent** — Avoid tagging, photographing, or sharing content involving classmates or peers without their explicit permission. |
| 6 | **Don't let it affect your mental health** — Avoid comparing your academic journey to others online; everyone's path is different. Unfollow accounts that make you feel inadequate. |

---

*Remember: Your digital footprint is permanent. Build it with intention.*
