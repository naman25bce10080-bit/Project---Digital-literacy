# 🛡️ Stay Safe Online — Prevention Checklist
### For College Students in India

> **Report cybercrime immediately at:** 🌐 [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) | 📞 Helpline: **1930**

---

## ✅ General Online Safety

- [ ] **1. Use strong, unique passwords for every account.**
  Create passwords with at least 12 characters mixing letters, numbers, and symbols.
  Use a password manager (e.g., Bitwarden, Google Password Manager) — never reuse passwords across sites.

- [ ] **2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all important accounts.**
  Turn on 2FA for email, social media, banking, and college portals.
  Prefer an authenticator app (Google Authenticator, Authy) over SMS-based OTPs where possible.

- [ ] **3. Never click suspicious links — verify before you tap.**
  Phishing messages often arrive via WhatsApp, Instagram DMs, or email disguised as scholarship offers,
  job alerts, or college notices. Always check the sender's real address/number before clicking any link.

- [ ] **4. Keep your devices and apps updated.**
  Security patches in updates fix vulnerabilities exploited by malware and ransomware.
  Enable auto-updates on your phone and laptop OS, and delete apps you no longer use.

- [ ] **5. Use a VPN and avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive tasks.**
  Never log in to banking, college ERP, or email on open café or college hotspot Wi-Fi.
  If you must use public Wi-Fi, always connect through a reputable VPN first.

- [ ] **6. Review app permissions regularly.**
  Many apps request access to contacts, camera, and location far beyond their need.
  Go to Settings → Apps → Permissions and revoke unnecessary access every few months.

---

## 💸 Financial & UPI Safety  *(Mandatory Focus)*

- [ ] **7. 🔴 NEVER enter your UPI PIN to *receive* money — it only sends money out.**
  Scammers pose as buyers (often as Army personnel or government officers on OLX/Quikr)
  and send a "collect request," tricking you into believing you're accepting a payment.
  **Rule:** Receiving money on UPI requires zero PIN entry. If someone asks you to enter your
  PIN to get paid, it is 100% a fraud — reject and report immediately.

- [ ] **8. 🔴 Verify any "bank/UPI customer care" contact through official channels only.**
  Fraudsters impersonate PhonePe, Google Pay, Paytm, or bank support on social media
  and WhatsApp. Never share your OTP, UPI PIN, card CVV, or full account number with anyone —
  **no genuine support agent will ever ask for these.** Always find helpline numbers from the
  bank's official website, not from Google search ads.

---

## 🤝 Social Media & Identity Safety

- [ ] **9. Set your social media profiles to private and audit friend/follower lists.**
  Oversharing your college name, hostel location, phone number, or daily routine
  makes you an easy target for stalkers, identity thieves, and social engineering attacks.

- [ ] **10. Do not share Aadhaar, PAN, or marksheet photos in unverified WhatsApp groups.**
  Many fake scholarship and internship scams harvest these documents.
  Share KYC documents only on official government portals (DigiLocker) or directly with verified institutions.

- [ ] **11. Think before you post — screenshots are permanent.**
  Cyberbullying and sextortion cases often begin with screenshots of private chats or images
  shared in trust. Once shared digitally, you lose control of that content forever.

---

## 🚨 What To Do If You Are a Victim

| Situation | Immediate Action |
|---|---|
| UPI / bank fraud | Call **1930** within minutes; also call your bank to freeze the transaction |
| Phishing / account hacked | Change passwords immediately, revoke all active sessions, alert your contacts |
| Cyberbullying / harassment | Screenshot evidence, block the user, report on the platform, then file at cybercrime.gov.in |
| Ransomware on device | Disconnect from Wi-Fi/data immediately; do NOT pay the ransom; seek IT help |
| Identity theft | File complaint at cybercrime.gov.in + nearest police station with a written complaint |
